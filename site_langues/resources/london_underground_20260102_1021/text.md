## Text

The London Underground is a famous train system in London, England. People also call it "the Tube." It started in 1863, making it very old. It was the first underground train system in the world. Today, the Tube has 11 lines and 270 stations. Many people use it every day to travel around the city.

The trains run underground in tunnels, and sometimes they come up above the ground. The Tube is very important for people who live in London. It helps them go to work, school, or visit friends. Tourists also use the Tube to see famous places, like the Big Ben, the London Eye, and Buckingham Palace.

Riding the Tube is easy. You need an Oyster card or a contactless bank card to pay for your trip. The Tube map is colorful and easy to understand. Each line has a different color. For example, the Central Line is red, and the Piccadilly Line is dark blue.

The trains are usually busy, especially in the morning and evening. People need to stand close to each other. Remember to be polite and let people get off the train before you get on. The Tube is a fast and convenient way to travel in London. It is a big part of daily life in the city.
