Christmas in the United States is a festive occasion marked by a multitude of traditions that both converge with and diverge from those in the United Kingdom. One of the most notable aspects of Christmas in the U.S. is the influence of its multicultural society, which brings a variety of customs to the holiday season, resulting in a distinctive celebration.

Americans often begin decorating their homes right after Thanksgiving, with an emphasis on colorful lights, wreaths, and elaborate lawn displays that sometimes attract tourists. Inside, Christmas trees are adorned with ornaments, tinsel, and lights, creating a warm and inviting atmosphere that is central to American Christmas celebrations.
